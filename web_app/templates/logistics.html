Logistics of Visiting Ecuador to Hike

Best Time of Year

The best time of year to hike in Ecuador is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. During this time, you can expect clear skies and comfortable temperatures, making for ideal hiking conditions. However, keep in mind that some popular trails, such as Cotopaxi, may be crowded during this time.

Main Airports

The two main airports in Ecuador are the Mariscal Sucre International Airport in Quito and the José Joaquín de Olmedo International Airport in Guayaquil. Quito is the most common point of entry for hikers, as it is located near many popular hiking destinations.

Weather

The weather in Ecuador can vary widely depending on the region and altitude. In general, however, the highlands are cooler and wetter than the coast. The temperature in Quito, for example, typically ranges from the mid-40s to mid-60s (Fahrenheit) throughout the year.

Culture and Main Things to Do

Ecuador is known for its rich culture and history, as well as its stunning natural beauty. Some of the main things to do in Ecuador include:

  • Visiting the Galapagos Islands to see unique wildlife
  • Exploring the historic city of Quito, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site
  • Hiking in the Andes Mountains, including popular peaks like Cotopaxi and Chimborazo
  • Experiencing indigenous culture in places like Otavalo, known for its colorful markets and traditional crafts
  • Tasting Ecuadorian cuisine, which features fresh seafood, roasted meats, and exotic fruits

Keep in mind that Ecuador is a Spanish-speaking country, so it can be helpful to know some basic Spanish phrases if you plan to travel outside of major tourist areas. It's also a good idea to research local customs and etiquette to ensure you're respectful of the culture during your visit.
