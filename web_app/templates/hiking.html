Hiking in Ecuador

Ecuador is a beautiful country with many opportunities for hiking. Here are five popular hikes to consider:

Chimborazo

Difficulty level: Difficult

Hours of hike: 8-12

Max altitude: 6,310 meters (20,702 feet)

Elevation gain: 1,400 meters (4,600 feet)

Drive from Quito: 3-4 hours

Cotopaxi

Difficulty level: Moderate to Difficult

Hours of hike: 6-8

Max altitude: 5,897 meters (19,347 feet)

Elevation gain: 900 meters (3,000 feet)

Drive from Quito: 1.5-2 hours

Illiniza Norte

Difficulty level: Moderate

Hours of hike: 4-6

Max altitude: 5,126 meters (16,818 feet)

Elevation gain: 850 meters (2,800 feet)

Drive from Quito: 2-3 hours

Ruco Pichincha

Difficulty level: Easy to Moderate

Hours of hike: 4-6

Max altitude: 4,698 meters (15,413 feet)

Elevation gain: 700 meters (2,297 feet)

Drive from Quito: 1 hour

These hikes offer a range of difficulty levels and stunning views of Ecuador's natural beauty. Remember to come prepared with appropriate gear and to acclimate yourself to the altitude before attempting any high-altitude hikes.
